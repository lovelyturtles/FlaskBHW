Do you ever feel ignored or belitted by healthcare professionals? Does it seem like they don't take your concerns seriously?

You are not alone.

One study found that women in emergency rooms are less likely to be taken seriously than men. Another study found that women often receive worse medical treatment than men with the same condition.

“Women have been more often referred to psychologists or psychiatrists, whereas men are given tests to rule out actual organic conditions”

- Christin Veasley, co-founder and director at the Chronic Pain Research Alliance

72% of the time, women in Denmark waited longer to be diagnosed than men.

39% of women who have a cardiac arrest in public were given CPR, compared to 45% of men.

Women are more likely to be given anti-anxiety medications than men when they come to a hospital with pain.

Our team at Doctors for Her have created this platform to connect women like you with doctors who will take your concerns seriously and give you the care you need.
